UCSD-Hyundai Robotaxi Project


From October 2018 to January 2019, I worked with the UCSD Design Lab in collaboration with Hyundai Motors to design in-transit activities for autonomous cars.

It was a huge project, involving over thirty students and staff, and I was involved in every step of the design process. This included some work in large groups, some work in small groups, and some things where I operated autonomously.

Data Collection and Synthesis

This is an example of some data synthesis. I helped consolodate footage, primarily taken on the metro, of what people do in-transit. I also helped collect observations in airports, Uber rides, and of people arriving on campus. We chose to conduct the majority of observations in places other than pritively owned cars, becuase we wanted to design for the activities you could do in autonomous cars of the future; things that people are not currently able to do.

Above is a photo I took while observing people waiting in the airport. There are a lot of interesting things to notice here when considering possible design implications of this observation. First, see how the father and daughter are both using mobile technology. As you can see in the first image, this is something that is very common. A good autonomous vehicle (AV) design should take this into account. Second, notice how one kid is in the father's lap. While this might not be ideal for AV's for safety reasons, it is certainly worth noting that this is something people would like to have. As well, the daughter is trying to show her screen to the father. An AV should probably have a seating arrangement that supports this activity sharing.
